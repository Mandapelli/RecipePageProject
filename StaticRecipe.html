Egg Curry

Egg Curry is a popular dish in Indian cuisine made with boiled eggs simmered in a flavorful tomato and onion-based gravy. It is a delicious, easy-to-make dish that can be enjoyed with rice, roti, or naan.

Ingredients

  • 4 eggs, boiled and peeled
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Water as needed

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add the finely chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown.
  3. Add the ginger-garlic paste and cook for 2-3 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Now add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and the oil starts to separate from the mixture.
  5. Stir in the turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Cook for a minute to release the flavors.
  6. Add a little water to the gravy to adjust its consistency and bring it to a simmer.
  7. Make small slits in the boiled eggs and gently add them to the curry.
  8. Cover and cook for 5-7 minutes, allowing the eggs to absorb the flavors of the curry.
  9. Sprinkle garam masala and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  10. Serve hot with rice, roti, or naan.
